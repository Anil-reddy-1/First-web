Modern tools

Modern agriculture tools have revolutionized farming, increasing efficiency and productivity. Tractors, harvesters, and precision farming technologies enable farmers to cultivate larger areas, reduce labor costs, and optimize resource use. Despite their clear benefits, many farmers, particularly in developing countries, remain unaware of the potential of these tools. This ignorance stems from limited access to information, lack of training, and financial constraints. Overcoming this gap is crucial to enhance agricultural output, improve livelihoods, and ensure food security for a growing global population.

Subsidy

The Indian government provides subsidies on modern agricultural tools to encourage their adoption among farmers. These subsidies aim to reduce the initial investment cost, making these technologies more accessible, especially for small and marginal farmers. This initiative promotes mechanization in agriculture, leading to increased productivity, reduced labor costs, and improved efficiency. Subsidies are often targeted towards specific tools and categories of farmers, with varying subsidy rates depending on the equipment and the farmer's socio-economic status.
